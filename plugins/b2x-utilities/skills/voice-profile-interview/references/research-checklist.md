# Research Checklist — Deep Pass

The goal of research is to answer as many interview questions as possible before asking the client. Every question answered here is a question the client does not have to sit through.

## Person track — sources to pull

Work through these in order. Stop when you have enough signal; you do not need every source.

1. **LinkedIn profile**
   - Headline and About section — how they describe themselves
   - Featured posts — pieces they are proud of
   - Activity feed — recent original posts (not reshares), last 20–30 posts if available
   - Recommendations they have written — shows their tone when being warm
2. **Personal website, Substack, Medium, or portfolio**
   - Homepage / About page — stated positioning
   - 3–5 most recent articles or essays
   - Bio copy
3. **B2X-held samples**
   - `Clients/[Client-Name]/brand-assets/` — any writing samples already collected
   - `Clients/[Client-Name]/Company-Profile-*.md` — if the subject is an executive of a B2X client
   - Past B2X deliverables written in their voice
4. **Third-party published work**
   - Guest articles, podcast transcripts, interviews — these often show voice more honestly than polished corporate content
5. **Video or audio transcripts** (if available)
   - Short clips from podcasts, webinars, conference talks
   - Useful because spoken rhythm often leaks into their writing

## Company track — sources to pull

1. **Company Profile**
   - `Clients/[Client-Name]/Company-Profile-[client-name]-YYYY-MM-DD.md`
   - This is often the single richest source because it already captures positioning and target audience
2. **Website**
   - Homepage — first-paragraph tone is the strongest signal
   - About / Our Story — brand personality narrative
   - Services or Products — how they talk about what they sell
   - Blog — pull 3–5 recent posts across authors to see voice consistency
   - Careers page — often has the most "human" voice
3. **LinkedIn company page**
   - About section
   - Most recent 10–15 posts
   - Employee advocacy posts if visible
4. **`Clients/[Client-Name]/brand-assets/`**
   - Existing style guides, brand books, tone documents
   - Press releases, decks, one-pagers
   - Any internal-voice or external-voice split that already exists
5. **Case studies and customer stories** — voice under constraints
6. **Email newsletters** — voice in a more informal channel
7. **Competitor voice sample** (1–2 competitors)
   - Not to copy, but to identify what the client wants to sound *different* from

## What to extract from each source

For every source, capture:

- **Tone markers** — formal/informal, warm/cool, assertive/hedged, playful/serious
- **Sentence length** — rough average, and whether length is consistent or varied
- **Opening patterns** — how pieces start (question, claim, story, context-setting)
- **Closing patterns** — how pieces end (CTA, summary, provocation, question)
- **Vocabulary they reach for** — recurring verbs, metaphors, industry terms
- **Vocabulary they avoid** — buzzwords absent from the corpus
- **Structure** — use of headers, lists, bullets, images, pull quotes
- **Formatting choices** — bold, italics, emphasis, emoji, exclamation points
- **Citation style** — inline, bracket, none
- **Audience posture** — do they write up, down, across to the reader
- **POV** — first person singular, first person plural, third person

## Build the research summary

After the sweep, produce a short (<1 page) summary organized as:

```
# Research Summary — [Client]

## Observed tone (3–5 adjectives)
...

## Sentence and rhythm signals
- Average length: ...
- Variation: ...

## Vocabulary signals
- Reaches for: ...
- Avoids: ...

## Structural patterns
- Openings: ...
- Closings: ...
- Lists/headers: ...

## Audience signals
...

## Content types observed
...

## Candidate "always" rules (from the work)
...

## Candidate "never" rules (from the work)
...

## Gaps research could not fill
(These become the questions you will actually ask the client.)
```

This summary is internal working material. It does not need to go into the final profile. It feeds Phase 3.

## When research is thin

Some clients are new, quiet online, or private. If you cannot find enough signal:

- Ask the user for 3–5 writing samples directly before starting the interview
- Add 2–3 "show me a piece you love / a piece you hate" questions to the interview
- Expect to land closer to the 25-question cap rather than the 10–15 floor

Do not fake signal. If you have no evidence for a field, mark it `[missing — must ask]` and ask.
